The MVP that never was...

Joe Burrow’s Historic 2024 Season: A Tale of Perseverance and Dominance

The 2024 season was a rollercoaster for Joe Burrow and the Cincinnati Bengals. What started as a frustrating campaign filled with missed opportunities turned into one of the most impressive individual runs in NFL history. Though the season ended in heartbreak, Burrow’s resilience and brilliance solidified his place among the league’s elite.

A Rocky Start

The Bengals, notorious for slow starts under Zac Taylor, stumbled out of the gate. Burrow struggled in the season opener as the Patriots pulled off a 16-10 upset. In Week 2, he rebounded in Kansas City, throwing for 258 yards and two touchdowns, but a costly turnover and controversial pass interference call doomed Cincinnati. Week 3 saw Burrow shine against Washington with 324 yards and three touchdowns, yet the defense faltered against eventual Offensive Rookie of the Year Jayden Daniels. The Bengals finally secured their first win of the season in Carolina, defeating former franchise QB Andy Dalton’s Panthers.

Close Calls & Costly Mistakes

Week 5 provided a masterclass performance from Burrow—nearly 400 yards and five touchdowns against Baltimore. Yet, an overtime loss, thanks to a late turnover and a missed field goal, left the Bengals reeling. They responded with wins against the Giants and Browns before running into the eventual Super Bowl champion Eagles, who blew them out.

Red-Hot Burrow, Failing Defense

The Bengals finally earned their first home win in dominant fashion, dismantling the Raiders as Burrow threw for 251 yards and five touchdowns. Another electrifying performance followed against Baltimore, where he posted a season-high 428 yards and four touchdowns, only to see another controversial officiating moment cost them a win. In a must-win Sunday Night Football matchup against the Chargers, the Bengals started slow, and even Burrow’s heroic second-half rally wasn’t enough. A Week 12 loss to Pittsburgh left Cincinnati needing a miracle to make the playoffs.

Burrow’s Legendary Streak

With everything on the line, Burrow caught fire. It started with a thrilling Monday night win in Dallas, where he threw for 369 yards and three touchdowns. The momentum continued as Cincinnati steamrolled the Titans and Browns, extending Burrow’s streak of 250+ yards and three-touchdown games to seven. Then came the Tee Higgins Game—a signature moment of the season. An acrobatic Higgins grab set up a first-and-goal before Burrow found him again for the overtime winner. Cincinnati closed the season with a hard-fought victory in Pittsburgh, though Burrow’s streak finally ended at eight games—an all-time NFL record.

Bengals Miss Playoffs, But Burrow’s Legacy Grows

Despite a five-game win streak to close the year, Cincinnati’s early-season struggles proved too costly. Their playoff hopes faded, but Burrow’s historic run will be remembered. Eight consecutive games with 250+ yards and three touchdowns is something the NFL may never see again. Though 2024 ended without a postseason berth, Burrow’s performance reaffirmed his place among the league’s best—and set the stage for an even bigger 2025.

Burrow finished the 2024 season with 4,918 yards, 45 total touchdowns, 9 interceptions, a completion percentage of 70.6, and a QBR of 74.7.
